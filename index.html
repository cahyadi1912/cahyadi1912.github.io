Child mortality in the past 2 decades

Child mortality in year 2000

This scatterplot shows an overview of child mortality in various countries around the world in year 2000, plotted against their respective GDP per capita. From this scatterplot it can be seen that there is a relationship between a country's GDP per capita and its child mortality. Countries with higher GDP per capita tend to have lower child mortality while the poorer countries with lower GDP per capita tend to have higher child mortality. From this scatter plot we can also see that Sub-Saharan African countries have the highest child mortality. European & Central Asian countries are located at the other end with higher GDP per capita and lower child mortality.

Progress of child mortality and GDP per capita

In the past 2 decades most countries have been progressing with declining number of child mortality and increasing GDP per capita. Since the child mortality numbers displayed here are the total mortality figures in each country, and not the mortality rate per certain number of birth or population, some countries with higher population appear in the chart as having high child mortality. This is the case for USA. Among the wealthy countries, USA has relatively high child mortality due its large population.

Causes of child mortality

As of year 2017 prematurity, accute lower respiratory infection, and birth asphyxia and birth trauma are the top 3 cause of child mortality. Certain diseases that were previously prominent 2 decades ago, such as diarrhoea diseases and malaria have dropped their ranks. The total mortality have dropped by 45.8% over the past 2 decades, bringing the child mortality level from 9 to 5 millions.
