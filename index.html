Child mortality in the past 2 decades

Child mortality in year 2000

This scatterplot shows the child mortality in different countries in year 2000, plotted against their respective GDP per Capita. It can be seen quite clearly that there is a relationship between a country's GDP per capita and its child mortality. Countries with higher GDP per capita tend to have lower child mortality; while the poorer countries with lower GDP per capita tend to have higher child mortality. The Sub SubSaharan African countries have the highest child mortality.

Progress of child mortality and GDP per capita

Since year 2000 most countries have been progressing with declining number of child mortality and increasing GDP per capita. Among high GDP per capita countries USA has higher child mortality due to its large population.

Cause of child mortality

As of year 2017 prematurity, accute lower respiratory infection, and birth asphyxia and birth trauma are the top 3 cause of child mortality. Certain diseases that were previously prominent 2 decades ago, such as diarrhoea diseases and malaria have dropped their ranks. The total mortality have dropped by 45.8% over the past 2 decades, bringing the child mortality level from 9 to 5 millions.
